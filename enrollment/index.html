The road to 30,000 students

Daniel Wheaton

Enrollment trends at UNL

Enrollment by college

After hitting near-record levels of enrollment last September, University of Nebraska-Lincoln officials remain cautiously optimistic about next year's incoming freshman class. The tail end of spring semester to the May deadline, is "crunch time" for admissions, because administrators say this is when students finalize their commitment to UNL, or decide to go somewhere else. The enrollment boosts are part of outgoing Chancellor Harvey Perlman's goal of having 30,000 students by 2020. Since UNL joined the Big Ten Conference in 2011, this opened up new opportunities for recruitment, as more students begin to see UNL as a comparative bargain to other comparable institutions. With the 25,006 students that were enrolled during the 2014-15 academic year, Perlman's goal is edging closer to reality. Last year, the College of Business Administration, and the College of Journalism and Mass Communication saw notable gains.

Enrollment forecast

Based on forecast data, a number of colleges may see major gains. The College of Education and Human sciences might become the second-largest college at UNL, overtaking the CBA. Many variables are at play, however.

CoJMC majors

Just because a college sees enrollment gains, it doesn't mean that it's evenly divided. Most of CoJMC's enrollment increases are because of Advertising and Public Relations. CoJMC Dean Maria Marron said she is refocusing recuitment efforts to help broadcasting grow. Because of this, CoJMC is also looking for a new ADPR professor.Since becoming dean last year, Marron has started to make up for the broadcasting decline by building partnerships with stations. With only three majors, CoJMC has the advantage of knowing its audience. Recruiters have been cooperating with Nebraska DECA and attending high school journalism conferences to get more out-of-state students. Faculty members have also been making calls to encourage students to come, which has paid dividends, Marron said. "It's the personal touch that often makes the difference," she said.
